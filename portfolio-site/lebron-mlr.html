NBA Contract Value Predictor

My piece of a group project: a multiple linear regression model that predicts a player's future LEBRON value, so a front office can judge whether a contract of the expected size is actually justified.

Purpose

Contracts are where front offices win and lose. The question I set out to answer was simple to state and hard to answer well: given how a player performs, what should he actually be worth, and does the contract he is likely to get make sense? LEBRON is a single, well regarded measure of a player's value, so I built a model to predict a player's future LEBRON from his current profile and use that as the yardstick.

Target audience

Front office decision makers weighing an extension or a free agent deal, who need a defensible number for what a player is worth rather than a gut feel.

Methods

I pulled current season per game and advanced stats from Basketball Reference, merged them with LEBRON values, and handled players who changed teams mid season. After feature selection I settled on thirteen inputs that balance scoring efficiency, playmaking, defense, usage, and value. I trained an ordinary least squares model on an 80 / 20 split and evaluated it with R squared and mean squared error on the held out set.

Results

The model produces a projected LEBRON for each player, which becomes a fair value estimate for a contract. Compare that projection against the deal on the table and you get a clean verdict: value, fair, or overpay.

Example model output

OLS regression summary

How to read it: R squared is the share of variation in LEBRON the model explains, so a test R squared near 0.8 means the inputs track real value well. Each coefficient is how much predicted LEBRON moves per unit of that stat, holding the others fixed. Efficiency and value metrics like WS/48 and VORP carry the most weight, which lines up with basketball intuition.

Predicted value vs the contract on the table

How to read it: the model's projected LEBRON becomes a fair salary estimate. Set it against the expected contract and the verdict falls out. Value means the player projects to outplay the deal, Overpay means the deal outruns the projection, and Fair means they roughly match. That is the exact call a front office is trying to make.

Projected vs actual LEBRON

How to read it: points near the dashed line are players the model nailed. A point well above the line is a player the model thinks is better than his current LEBRON suggests, a natural buy low candidate. Below the line is the opposite, a possible sell high.
