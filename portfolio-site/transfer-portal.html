Transfer Portal Performance Prediction

Two predictive models that surface the handful of variables that separate transfer pitchers, with Self Organizing Maps used to reveal release angle archetypes across the portal.

Role
Analyst
Stack
R · Python · Kohonen SOM · Plotly
Audience
Recruiting and pitching staff
Scale
200+ NCAA pitching transfers
All figures use dummy data. The modeling approach and the Self Organizing Map are the real design. UVA's underlying data is proprietary and is not present here.

Purpose

The transfer portal moves fast and the stakes are high. A staff needs to know, quickly, which incoming arms are likely to help and why. I built models that identify the small set of variables that actually predict transfer performance, so recruiting decisions lean on signal instead of reputation.

Target audience

The recruiting and pitching staff making portal decisions on a deadline. The deliverable has to be fast to read and defensible when a coach asks why the model likes a player.

Methods

I built two predictive models that each narrow a wide dataset down to five to ten key variables for evaluating a pitcher. Alongside them I used Self Organizing Maps to compress each pitcher's release profile onto a two dimensional map, which groups arms into release angle archetypes and makes similar pitchers land near each other. Dynamic visualizations let the staff compare an incoming transfer against the archetype he most resembles.

Results

The models turn a sprawling portal into a short, ranked shortlist with a clear rationale for each name, and the archetype map gives the staff an intuitive way to say who a new pitcher pitches like.

Deliverable 1 · dummy data

Release angle archetype map

How to read it: the Self Organizing Map lays every pitcher on a grid so that similar release profiles sit next to each other. Each colored region is an archetype of arm slot and release angle. When a transfer maps into a region, you instantly know which of your current pitchers he throws like, and what has worked or not worked for that group.
Deliverable 2 · dummy data

Transfer projection model

Key predictors the model kept
How to read it: the bars are the five to ten variables the model kept after selection, the ones that actually move the projection. The table ranks incoming transfers by projected next season value, with a fit grade for how cleanly each maps onto a proven archetype. A high projection with a high fit is the safest bet, a high projection with a low fit is the high upside gamble.
